My Story



 I was born and raised in Kansas and I've always had a passion for learning why things work. As a kid, I loved to tear things apart to see if I could put them back together. This quickly lead to a hobby of puzzles and problem-solving. When I was younger I did all kinds of puzzles like word searches and sudoku. After some time I got into taking things apart and working on them. It was beneficial that I lived on a farm because there was something to work on at every turn.

 In my later teen years, I picked the hobbies of chess and Rubik's cubes. These quickly became an obsession of mine. I learned how to solve the 2x2, 3x3, 4x4, 5x5, and 6x6 Rubiks cubes in less than a year. I even learned how to solve the 2x2 and 3x3 blindfolded. I also climbed the ranks of chess into the 1800 Elo rating.

What stopped me from these obsessions? Programming.

 I took a computer class that was programming for one semester and computer applications for the second. I immediately knew I had found a career for myself. The problem-solving with challenges at every turn made me excited to go into school. Unfortunately, I did not jump right into programming. I was a full-time student working a full-time job and I had required classes to finish the year as the current school I went to had limited computer, science classes.

  I moved to another college that offered more programming and mathematical course. I wanted to do programming, but I also had a love for math that could not be ignored. That's when I found computer science. It's a good mix of both that is challenging and intellectually stimulating.

  After finding computer science, I dove into programming headfirst with Java. I used school and self-education to learn Java. My main resource outside of school to learn Java was Tim Balchalka's Udemy course Java Masterclass.

  Quickly after getting my Associates in General Science and Liberal Arts, I moved on to Western Governors University. My goal for this college was graduate in half the time and work twice as hard. I had a weekend full-time job that allowed me to support myself and my family while simultaneously putting in the work at school. It was a rough year with a lot of sleepless nights, but I obtained my degree in 12 months or 2 terms. I graduated with my Bachelor's in Computer Science in December of 2021! << hover over me

  Now, I spend my free time away from work working on side projects and side education to better myself in any way I can. My goal is to become a Software Engineer. and I won't stop until I have achieved that goal. I have put in a ton of effort and never want to stop learning and growing. After becoming an SWE, I want to work even harder to climb the ladder and become a Principle Software Engineer.
